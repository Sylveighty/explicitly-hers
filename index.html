The Woman Behind the Story

Kathlen Mae Callos

BSIT 2-1 · Member, EXPLICIT Organization

A friendly and caring soul, she moves through the world with warmth and trustworthiness. A student of information technology and a member of EXPLICIT, she carries her love for both hardware and code - and the quiet courage to learn what she doesn't yet know.

Her Journey

Chapters of Becoming

Chapter One

A Spark in the Classroom

Long before college, a TVE class in high school opened the door. ICT became a world she wanted to live in - from senior high to BSIT, every step was guided by that early curiosity about technology and what it could do.

Chapter Two

When the Code Didn't Speak Back

Hardware was familiar. Programming was not. The struggle was real: staring at code that felt like a foreign language, feeling the weight of self-doubt. But her explorer's spirit pushed her forward. She put in the time, sat with the difficulty, and kept going.

Chapter Three

Finding Her Voice in the Community

EXPLICIT didn't just give her a membership. It gave her growth. Being part of the organization challenged her to speak up, collaborate, and connect. Social communication, once daunting, became a skill she slowly and steadily made her own.

Chapter Four: The Breakthrough

"Trust GOD, and Nothing is Impossible with Him"

She doubted herself. Could she really compete in the Philippine History Quiz Bee and represent EXPLICIT? With faith, effort, and time - she qualified. She competed. She came back with lessons, memories, and a heart full of gratitude. It was more than a competition. It was proof.

Looking Forward

Building What Comes Next

She's not stopping here. Hackathons, better systems, and another Quiz Bee. This time for Q4E. She carries her experience forward, with her faith, her curiosity, and her drive to represent and to build.

"

Explore more, and discover something better.

Kathlen Mae Callos · BSIT 2-1, EXPLICIT

A Tribute

A Letter to Her

Dear Callos,

Sitting in the same class as you, I've seen something in you that I don't think you give yourself enough credit for - you try things. Even when you're not sure. Even when it's uncomfortable. Most people wait until they feel ready. You just go.

I watched you go from someone who knew hardware but not code, to someone who actually sat down and learned it. I saw you sign up for the Quiz Bee even when you weren't sure you belonged there. And then you qualified. You competed. And you came back different , a little more sure of yourself, less afraid of the next thing. That's not luck. That's just you, doing what you always do.

We're in the same class but honestly, Callos, you've taught me something. Keep going. There's a lot more you haven't tried yet.

From a classmate who's watching,
